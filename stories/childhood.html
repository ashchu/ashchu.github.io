When we grow up.


I loved stories. Grew up with them. Read countless. Watched many more. And went through all the waves...Wattpad, Fanfics, John Greene, and complaining about film adaptations.' So naturally, I wanted my own life to be a picture I captured of sorts.

Whenever someone asks about the spark or key moment where I knew I was interested in CS, I start with a story.

I'm not sure what it was... maybe I should read through the now dusty notebooks somewhere cluttered on my shelf to find out... but I wanted an escape from the world. My perception of perfection started in either 4th or 5th grade right near when my brother was growing fast and I was no longer the baby. My personality at the time was one geared towards perfection. I was trained on barbies and polly pockets to see that my idea of myself was to be a quirky, pretty, preferably blonde-haired, size 0 girl that lived luxuriously. Though, as a total aside... I did have that stereotypical experience where I eventually did steal my brother's LEGOs.

So on this night at 4AM in the morning I had a realization on how my childhood favorite game actually influenced my life and decisions growing up. For a while, I would innocently share this story with others as it truly did inspire me and displaced me out of the real world and into a virtual realistic world where I could be my true and best self ... or what I thought that was. To you wonderful readers, I'll share this story again with calling out the systematic roles I learned through my favorite video game Fantage.

Today Fantage is gone and although I do have mixed feelings towards this game today, I will say it was a huge highlight of my good ol' middle school years..

Fantage was my perfect world. Running home after school and zooming onto my dad's clunky HP laptop, I would log-in to ensure I could catch the latest styles, coins, outfits, and check-in with my friends. It was almost perfect. Here, I could be quirky, beautiful, and cool with the touch of a membership fee and a few connections you could become popular. A concept I never really fathomed in the real world.
